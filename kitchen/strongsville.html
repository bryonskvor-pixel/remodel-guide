Every Level of Remodel

We cover every level — so whether you want a weekend refresh or a complete transformation, there's a real option. The in-home visit tells us which one fits your kitchen and your budget.

Level 01

Cabinet Tune-Up

New doors, new hardware, new look — without replacing your existing cabinet boxes. Fastest turnaround, lowest disruption. Most Strongsville kitchens qualify.

Right for you if: Your cabinet boxes are solid but the doors and hardware feel dated. You want a noticeable change without the cost of full replacement.
Done in days
Level 02

Cabinet Refacing

Keep your existing box layout, replace all the surfaces — doors, drawer fronts, and exposed frames. A completely new look at a fraction of full replacement cost.

Right for you if: Your layout works, your boxes are sound, but everything you see looks wrong. Refacing is the smartest spend for kitchens like that.
Most popular
Level 03

Full Cabinet Replacement

All new cabinets, new layout options, new storage configuration. The right choice when your boxes are damaged, your layout doesn't work, or you're ready to go custom.

Right for you if: You want to change where things are — move the island, add an upper run, eliminate the soffit. New boxes give you that freedom.
Layout changes possible
Level 04

Complete Renovation

Cabinets, countertops, backsplash, lighting, flooring — the whole kitchen, done together. For Strongsville colonials and split-levels that are ready for a real transformation.

Right for you if: The layout, the finishes, the lighting — all of it needs to change. This is the project that makes the kitchen feel like a different house.

Strongsville Specifically

We know what's behind these walls.

Strongsville built fast between 1970 and 2000. Colonials in Ledgewood and Deerfield Lake, split-levels along Albion Road, larger transitional homes in the High Point corridors. Almost all of it built with builder-grade kitchens that were never meant to last 30 years without an update.

The profile is consistent across the older stock: soffits that box in the ceiling, original laminate or dated countertops, raised-panel oak cabinet doors, and a closed-off layout designed for a different decade. The bones are excellent. The kitchens just need to catch up.

The 1996–2011 generation — the larger colonials near High Point, the transitional homes along the southern corridors — are hitting their 20–25 year window right now. Original builder-grade finishes, original cabinet boxes. This is exactly the moment when a kitchen update makes both practical and financial sense. The equity is there. The timing is right.

We've been in enough of these homes to know the difference between a kitchen that needs a refresh and one that needs a rethink. The visit is how we figure out which one yours is.

Common Questions

What Strongsville homeowners ask first.

How much does a kitchen remodel cost in Strongsville?
It depends entirely on scope and level — which is exactly why we start with a free in-home visit rather than a number on a website. A cabinet tune-up is a very different investment than a complete renovation. The visit gives you a written estimate based on your actual kitchen, not a guess.
Do I have to visit a showroom to get started?
No. The in-home conversation comes first — we come to you. Showroom visits for cabinets and countertops happen later in the process, when you're ready to see real materials and make selections. We'll schedule those with you and go along when the time comes.
My house was built in the 1980s. Is that a problem for a remodel?
Not at all — it's the most common situation we work with in Strongsville. Ledgewood, Deerfield Lake, and the original colonial and ranch tracts from that era are exactly what we do. The structural framing is solid. What typically needs updating is the kitchen layout, the cabinet faces, and the countertops. All of that is straightforward work.
Can you open up my kitchen and remove a wall?
Yes. Opening a kitchen to the dining or family room is one of the most common requests we handle in Strongsville — especially in the split-level and closed-concept colonial layouts from the 70s and 80s. We assess the wall situation during the in-home visit. Load-bearing walls require a structural approach that we coordinate as part of the project.
How long does a kitchen remodel take?
A cabinet tune-up with new doors can be done in days. A refacing project runs one to two weeks. Full cabinet replacement is two to four weeks. A complete renovation — countertops, flooring, lighting, layout changes — depends on scope but plan for four to eight weeks including lead times. You get a written timeline before any work starts.
What's the difference between this and calling a contractor directly?
When you call a contractor, you're talking to someone with one answer. We cover every level — from a cabinet refresh to a full gut renovation — so the recommendation you get is based on what your kitchen actually needs, not what a particular company happens to sell. Bryon has 20 years of construction experience. You're getting a real assessment, not a pitch.
