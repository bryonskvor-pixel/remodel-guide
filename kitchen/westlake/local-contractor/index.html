Why a Cabinet Specialist Beats a General Contractor

Most kitchen projects in Westlake don't need heavy construction.

General contractors are built for structural work — moving walls, rerouting plumbing, pulling permits. That overhead adds cost and time to projects that never needed any of it. Common Questions

What Westlake homeowners ask me first.

What's the difference between what you do and a general contractor?
A general contractor manages structural projects — wall removal, plumbing relocation, electrical changes, permits. I specialize in the surfaces: cabinet painting, refacing, countertops, backsplash. If your kitchen layout works and you just want it to look better, a GC adds cost and time you don't need. If you're moving walls or changing the footprint, I'm not the right person — but that's a different project.
Do cosmetic kitchen updates require permits in Westlake?
No. Cabinet painting, refacing, countertop replacement, and backsplash tile don't involve structural changes, plumbing rerouting, or electrical work. No permit is required. The entire project stays inside your existing kitchen footprint.
How much does a kitchen refresh cost in Westlake?
It depends entirely on what you want to do. Cabinet painting alone can run $3,000–$8,000. Refacing runs $15,000–$32,000 depending on kitchen size and materials. A full refresh with new countertops and backsplash can run $25,000–$50,000 — still far less than a structural remodel. I'll give you an exact number when I see your kitchen.
Do I have to use a specific brand or supplier?
For cabinet work, I'm affiliated with Kitchen Tune-Up, which gives access to a large catalog of door styles, finishes, and materials. For countertops and backsplash, we work from local fabricators and tile suppliers. If you have something specific in mind, I'll let you know if we can source it and what it costs.
