The Three Appliance Integration Pitfalls We Eliminate

Working cleanly inside your kitchen's existing structural frame requires strict attention to appliance interfaces. Here is how layout preservation experts prevent delivery day disasters:

1 · The Refrigerator Depth Protrusion

Standard retail refrigerators stand 34 to 36 inches deep, causing them to awkwardly bulge past your 24-inch cabinet lines. We carefully track counter-depth model specs, and can modify upper cabinet headers on-site to create an executive, integrated built-in frame aesthetic.

2 · Slide-In Range Plane Gaps

Swapping a legacy drop-in cooktop or dated freestanding unit for a modern slide-in range requires precision edge work. We cut your solid quartz countertop slabs to interface seamlessly with the range's flange lips, preventing grease traps and unaligned surface lines.

3 · Ventilation Clearance Violations

Upgrading over-the-range microwaves or adding high-CFM custom range hoods often violates strict fire safety clearances to your cooking plane. We calculate cabinet bottom adjustments before crafting doors to guarantee code safety compliance.

Integration FAQ

Frequently Asked Sizing Questions

What is the difference between a standard refrigerator and a counter-depth model?
A standard freestanding refrigerator stands roughly 32 to 36 inches deep, causing it to push far past your kitchen's 24-inch cabinet box line. A counter-depth model optimizes its box depth to roughly 24 to 25 inches, allowing only the door profile and handles to extend past the cabinet frames for a cleaner look. When we reface your cabinets, we look at these dimensions to make sure the surrounding box frames look intentional.
Can you modify my existing cabinet heights to fit taller modern refrigerators?
Yes, this is an entry pillar of our layout-preservation methodology. If your sound existing framework is perfectly stable but your overhead bridge cabinet hangs too low to accommodate a modern 70-inch high refrigerator model, our trim carpenters cut down and modify that specific box unit on-site. We then skin it in premium wood veneers to match your brand-new shaker doors.
When should I purchase my new kitchen appliances?
Always select and lock down your exact model numbers *before* your final cabinetry refacing and countertop measurement sheets are finalized. Having your exact appliance spec sheet allows us to check every clearance margin ahead of time, ensuring a stress-free installation day.
