Why I do this

Twenty years inside the industry.
Always watching homeowners get lost.

I've spent more than twenty years in residential construction — cabinetry shops, flooring showrooms, design-build teams. I've seen the inside of more kitchens than I can count, and I've watched homeowners get overwhelmed, over-sold, and under-prepared at every step.

The problem is never the contractor. The problem is that homeowners walk into this process without a professional standing. The showroom wants to move product. The contractor needs to close jobs. Nobody in that chain is there just to help the homeowner understand what they actually need.

That's what I'm here for. I come to your home, I look at your actual kitchen, and I tell you honestly what's possible at your budget — from a cabinet tune-up to a full renovation. No upsell agenda. No showroom appointment. Just a real conversation with someone who knows what's behind your walls.

Background

20+ Yrs

Residential & Light Commercial Construction

Cabinetry, flooring, design-build, and showrooms across Northeast Ohio. Not a sales background — a field background. I've been in the homes, seen what goes wrong during installation, and learned how to catch those problems before any money is spent.

PMP

Project Management Professional Certification

The PMP is the gold standard in project management — demonstrated experience running real projects, verified by examination. For a kitchen remodel, it means thinking in scope, sequence, risk, and timeline instead of just materials and labor. It's why I can walk into a kitchen and see a project instead of just a room.

Cabinets

Deep Expertise in the Most Complex Kitchen Decision

Cabinets drive every other decision in a kitchen — countertop dimensions, appliance specs, lighting placement, the whole sequence. I know cabinet construction, finish options, lead times, and installation at a professional level. That means real guidance, not showroom sales language.

Flooring

LVP, Hardwood, Tile, Carpet — No Sales Agenda

I've sold every flooring material, installed it, and seen it fail when the wrong product went in the wrong place. That experience is what makes the guidance honest. If LVP makes sense for your kitchen, I'll tell you. If it doesn't, I'll tell you that too.

NEO

I Know These Homes Specifically

Most of the homes I work in were built between 1985 and 2010 by national builders — and I know what that means: builder-grade cabinets, laminate countertops, basic layouts that are completely functional but never felt like yours. These are exactly the kitchens that respond best to a smart, focused remodel.
