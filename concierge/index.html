What is a
Remodeling Concierge?

People need someone to talk to about their project before they need someone to sell them something. Every contractor who walks into a home has something to sell. Every showroom has product to move. Every designer has their taste to express. A Remodeling Concierge is the one conversation where nobody profits from what you decide — only from whether you're clear when you decide it.

Why This Role Exists

Every person in the remodeling process
has something to sell.

"The contractor sells labor. The showroom sells product. The designer sells taste. The window company sells windows. Nobody is just in the homeowner's corner — giving honest information with nothing to gain from what you decide."

This is not a criticism of contractors. A small contractor has to close — that is the economic reality of their business. A showroom exists to move product. A designer builds a portfolio. Every one of these people can be honest and excellent at their job while still being motivated by an outcome that is different from yours.

The homeowner is the only person in the remodeling process with no professional standing. They walk into contractor meetings unprepared, into showrooms without a direction, and into decisions without the context to evaluate what they're being told. The result is scope creep, budget shock, design regret, and the quiet feeling that nobody was really listening.

A Remodeling Concierge exists to change the position of the homeowner in that process. Not by replacing contractors or designers — they are essential. By ensuring that the homeowner shows up to every one of those conversations from a position of knowledge, clarity, and confidence rather than confusion and vulnerability.

What Happens During a Visit

60 to 90 minutes.
No pitch. No bid. No agenda except yours.

The Concierge visit follows the Remodelry Method — a structured, emotionally intelligent process built on 20+ years of residential construction field experience. Here is what happens, phase by phase.

01
Listen
The Concierge arrives already knowing your project — Remi captured it. They confirm, deepen, and listen for what the conversation may have missed. Your goals, your priorities, what success actually looks like to you.
02
Inspect
They look at what's actually there — existing conditions, potential issues, what the space can and can't do. No selling. Just seeing clearly. The things that get discovered here protect you later.
03
Measure
Accurate dimensions, photos, and documentation of the space as it exists today. The kind of detail that makes every contractor conversation sharper — because you arrive with documentation they don't have to create.
04
Clarify
What's realistic. What the process looks like. What things cost in honest terms. What to watch for. Knowledge before decisions — not after you've already committed to something.
05
Document
Everything captured in the Clarity Package — yours to keep and use however you choose. Share it with any contractor. Take it to any showroom. Use it to ask the questions that unprepared homeowners never think to ask.
06
Advise
Plain-language guidance on next steps, questions to ask every contractor, what good bids look like, and how to move forward with confidence. The conversation you needed before anyone else showed up.
The Clarity Package

What you leave with.
Yours to keep — regardless of what comes next.

The Clarity Package is the output of every Concierge visit. A plain-language document built around your specific project, your specific space, and your specific situation. Not a template. A real assessment of your real project — written in language you can hand directly to a contractor.

There is no obligation attached to receiving it. You can take it and source your own contractors. You can use it as the foundation for the next phase of the Remodelry process. Or you can simply use it to feel clear and confident about a project you've been putting off for too long.

Clarity Package
Your project, understood.
01

Your Project Story

What we heard, what we understood, and what we believe is driving this project. Your emotional driver. What success looks like to you. Written in your own words — so it feels right before anything else is built on top of it.

02

Who's Involved

Decision makers, communication preferences, and your SoulMap profile — how you make decisions, what you need from the process, how a contractor should communicate with you. Written in plain language that travels with the project.

03

Your Space — What We Found

Overall condition, accurate measurements, infrastructure observations, photo documentation. The before — captured before anything changes. Every contractor you share this with starts from the same accurate baseline.

04

Your Project Scope

What this project is, in plain language. Room by room — what's in, what's out, what's explicitly not in scope, what's still undecided. No ambiguity. No assumptions. A scope document that prevents the most expensive remodeling conversation — the one that starts with "I thought that was included."

05

Budget Reality

Honest assessment of whether your stated budget aligns with your scope and finish level. What drives the number. What could be phased. How to read estimates when they arrive — so a wildly low bid and a wildly high bid are both worth questioning.

06

What to Watch For

The flags from this visit — plain language, what it is, why it matters, what to do about it. The unknowns behind walls. Permit requirements. Lead time flags. Named upfront so they don't surprise you later.

07

Your Design Direction

Style in your own words. Color direction. The anchor piece — the one element that defines the space that everything else should build around. Inspiration references. Open decisions still to be made.

08

Decisions to Make

Every decision that needs to happen — and when. Some before estimates. Some before ordering. Some during the project. Organized by stage, not by stress. So you're never surprised by a decision that needed to be made two weeks ago.

09

Your Next Steps

Specific actions before estimates. Questions to ask every contractor — tailored to your project and your flags. What happens next with Remodelry, if you choose to go further. Written as actions, not suggestions.

The Clarity Package belongs to you. Share it with any contractor you choose. Use it independently. Take it to a showroom. Or use it as the foundation for the next phase of the Remodelry process. There is no obligation to do anything with it except use it to your advantage.
